Bandwiden Launches Pilot Program in Rural Montana

Posted on June 15, 2024 by Sarah Johnson, Project Lead

We're excited to announce the launch of our first pilot program in rural Montana, bringing the power of Bandwiden's CommunityCache to the small town of Pine Creek.

Pine Creek, with a population of just over 500, has long struggled with limited internet connectivity. Many residents have had to rely on slow, expensive satellite internet or travel to nearby towns for basic online tasks. Our team has been working closely with local community leaders and the regional ISP to implement a network of CommunityCache devices throughout the town.

The initial results have been promising:

  • Local students now have access to a wide range of educational resources, including video lectures and interactive learning materials, without the need for constant high-speed internet.
  • The town's small businesses are benefiting from improved access to online marketplaces and business resources.
  • Residents are enjoying entertainment options previously unavailable due to bandwidth limitations.

John Doe, Pine Creek's mayor, shared his thoughts: "Bandwiden's technology has been a game-changer for our community. It's like we've finally joined the 21st century. Our kids have better educational opportunities, and our local businesses can compete on a more level playing field."

This pilot program is just the beginning. We're closely monitoring the impact and gathering data to refine our technology further. The insights gained from this real-world implementation will be invaluable as we prepare to scale our solution to other underserved communities.

We'd like to thank the people of Pine Creek for their enthusiasm and cooperation, as well as our dedicated team and partners who made this pilot possible. Stay tuned for more updates as we continue to bridge the digital divide, one community at a time.
